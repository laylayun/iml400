History of Virtual Pets: A Timeline

1996

Tamagotchi

Tamagotchi, meaning "egg watch" in Japanese, is a handheld digital pet game. Players, mostly children, are responsible for taking care of the virtual pet by feeding it, playing games, and keeping it clean. It was a popular toy in the late 1990s and early 2000s both in Japan and the US.

  • Purpose: Growth
  • Device: Handheld device, created for the game
  • Developer: WiZ and Bandai
  • Maintnance Level: 5
  • Fun Fact: Aki Maita, employee at Bandai, came up with the idea of Tamagotchi because she wanted to make a pet for kids without the responsibility of cleaning up after them, dealing with loud noises, or paying large vet bills

1997

Digimon

As an extension of the virtual pet series, Digimon was created by the manufacturers of Tamagotchi with the intention of targeting male children. This virtual pet later evolved into a franchise, expanding into anime and video games. While the original virtual pet game focused on the growth of the pet, the franchise later became more oriented towards training and competition.

  • Purpose: Growth
  • Device: Handheld device, same format as Tamagotchi
  • Developer: WiZ and Bandai
  • Maintnance Level: 5
  • Fun Fact: According to Nielsen ratings, in May 2000, Digimon was more popular than Pokemon

1999

Neopets

Neopets is a virtual pet website. Players could take care of their virtual pets, or "Neopets," by buying virtual items using virtual currencies. The virtual currencies could be earned through playing games or interacting with other players.

  • Purpose: Growth
  • Device: Website
  • Developer: Nickelodeon
  • Maintnance Level: 3
  • Fun Fact: Neopets themselves can have pets, which are called "petpets." Petpets can also have pets, which are called "petpetpets."

2005

Nintendogs

In Nintendogs, players can raise, train, and play with virtual dogs. This game became very popular by using the Nintendo DS's microphone, internal clock, and the dual touchscreen to give players a realistic pet-raising experience.

  • Purpose: Growth
  • Device: Nintendo DS
  • Developer: Nintendo
  • Maintnance Level: 4
  • Fun Fact: If the player doesn't play the game for a long period of time, the dog may run away

Webkinz

Webkinz is a virtual pet website where players can raise the pets and decorate their homes. Ganz, the developer of the game, also sold real-life plushies, which came with an access code that allowed players to enjoy more features on the website.

  • Purpose: Growth
  • Device: Website
  • Developer: Ganz
  • Maintnance Level: 3
  • Fun Fact: The most expensive Webkinz toy is Cheeky Dog, which costs about $1,800

2014

Neko Atsume

In Neko Atsume, players can players can place food and toys in the virtual garden to attract different types of cats. Players do not "own" any of the cats.

  • Purpose: Relaxation
  • Device: Mobile app
  • Developer: Hit Point Co. Ltd
  • Maintnance Level: 1
  • Fun Fact: Yutaka Takasaki, the developer of the game, said "to be honest, I do not know why this game is so popular"

2016

Tabby Cat

Tabby Cat is a Chrome Extension that gives a new cat when a user opens new tab. Users can collect accessories and dress up the cats, but there are no maintenance necessary.

  • Purpose: Relaxation
  • Device: Chrome Extension
  • Developer: Leslie Stet
  • Maintnance Level: 1
  • Fun Fact: Tabby Cat was released when most popular Chrome extensions were targeted towards productivity, like to-do lists and note apps. Stet made Tabby Cat to feel happy and relaxed, which is almost counter-productive.

2019

Touchbat Pets

Touchbar Pets is a virtual pet that lives on MacBook touchbar. Initially, there weren't any features but as it became more popular, Popa added features like shoppibng, walking, and challenges.

  • Purpose: Relaxation
  • Device: MacBook Touch Bar
  • Developer: Sam Chiet
  • Maintnance Level: 1
  • Fun Fact: There have been over 1.7 million downloads

2020

Desktop Goose

Desktop Goose is a freeware that creates chaos on desktop, like tracking mud, stealing the mouse, writing notes, and bringing random goose images.

  • Purpose: Entertainment
  • Device: Desktop
  • Developer: Sam Chiet
  • Maintnance Level: 3
  • Fun Fact: Desktop Goose attacks you if you poke him

2021

Finch

Finch is a pet app with an emphasis on self-care. To take care of the finch, players must take care of themselves first through completing self-care tasks like reflections and breathing exercises.

  • Purpose: Relaxation
  • Device: Mobile app
  • Developer: Finch Care Public Benefit Corporation
  • Maintnance Level: 3
  • Fun Fact: Finch is charted as #16 on Apple App Store's Health& Fitness Category
